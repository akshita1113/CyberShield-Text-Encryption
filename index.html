▸ HOW EACH ALGORITHM WORKS

    

      

        
AES-256

        
Advanced Encryption Standard. Uses the same key for encrypt & decrypt (symmetric). 256-bit key means 2²⁵⁶ possible keys — unbreakable by brute force. Used by governments and banks worldwide.

      

      

        
DES / 3DES

        
Data Encryption Standard (1977). Original 56-bit DES was cracked in 1999. Triple-DES applies it 3× for stronger encryption. Now deprecated in favor of AES. Important for understanding crypto history.

      

      

        
RSA

        
Asymmetric encryption using two keys: public (share freely) + private (keep secret). Based on difficulty of factoring large prime numbers. Used in HTTPS, SSH, digital signatures, and key exchange.
